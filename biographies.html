Medieval

Early Classical

Joseph Bodin de Boismortier

Short bio Long bio

1689–1755 – French composer who bypassed aristocratic patronage by writing highly accessible instrumental music, helping shape a new commercial market for elegant, tuneful works aimed at amateurs and domestic performance.

Carlo Tessarini

Short bio Long bio

c.1690–1766 – Italian violinist-composer whose works sit between late Baroque virtuosity and early Classical balance, favouring lyrical expression over excessive technical display.

Giuseppe Tartini

Short bio Long bio

1692–1770 – Italian violin virtuoso and theorist whose emotionally charged style, advanced bowing techniques, and pedagogical influence reshaped violin performance across Europe.

Giovanni Battista Sammartini

Short bio Long bio

c.1700–1775 – Italian composer regarded as a key architect of the early symphony, replacing Baroque texture with clear thematic development and forward-driving structure.

Pietro Nardini

Short bio Long bio

1722–1793 – Italian violinist and composer from the Tartini school, noted for refining violin technique while embracing the emerging Classical emphasis on melodic elegance.

Giuseppe Sarti

Short bio Long bio

1729–1802 – Italian opera composer whose clear melodic writing and dramatic instincts made him influential both in Italy and at the Russian imperial court.

Joseph Haydn

Short bio Long bio

1732–1809 – Austrian composer who systematised the Classical style, defining the symphony and string quartet through formal clarity, humour, and structural innovation.

Luigi Boccherini

Short bio Long bio

1743–1805 – Italian composer and cellist whose refined chamber music blends Classical balance with a uniquely intimate, lyrical, and Mediterranean sensibility.

Classical

🎻 Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart

Short bio Long bio

1756–1791 – Austrian composer whose effortless melodic invention and absolute formal control defined Classical music at its peak, mastering every genre with deceptive ease while embedding emotional and psychological depth beneath flawless surfaces.

🎻 Ludwig van Beethoven

Short bio Long bio

1770–1827 – German composer who shattered Classical restraint and rebuilt musical form through force of will, expanding scale, harmony, and emotional scope to create a new model of artistic authority that permanently altered Western music.

🎻 Franz Schubert

Short bio Long bio

1797–1828 – Austrian composer whose unmatched gift for melody transformed song, chamber music, and the symphony into vehicles of profound lyricism, emotional vulnerability, and quiet existential depth.

Romantic

🎻 Frédéric Chopin

Short bio Long bio

1810–1849 – Polish composer and pianist whose revolutionary piano writing fused poetic intimacy with radical harmonic language, redefining the expressive limits of a single instrument.

🎻 Franz Liszt

Short bio Long bio

1811–1886 – Hungarian virtuoso, showman, and visionary who expanded pianistic technique, pioneered the symphonic poem, and bridged Romantic excess with modern harmonic thinking.

🎻 Bedřich Smetana

Short bio Long bio

1824–1884 – Founder of Czech national music, best known for programmatic orchestral works that fuse folklore, history, and political identity with forward-driving narrative force.

🎻 Johann Strauss II

Short bio Long bio

1825–1899 – The Waltz King, whose irresistibly elegant dance music transformed Viennese popular entertainment into an international musical language.

🎻 Alexander Borodin

Short bio Long bio

1833–1887 – Russian composer and chemist, a key member of the 'Mighty Handful', whose richly lyrical and exotic style blended nationalist colour with sweeping melodic beauty, most famously in 'Prince Igor' and its Polovtsian Dances.

🎻 Johannes Brahms

Short bio Long bio

1833–1897 – German composer and pianist whose masterful synthesis of Classical form and Romantic expression produced works of profound structural integrity and emotional depth, securing his place as one of the great symphonists after Beethoven.

🎻 Camille Saint-Saëns

Short bio Long bio

🎻 Georges Bizet

🎻 Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky

Short bio Long bio

🎻 Antonín Dvořák
